\section{Exercise 3: Ising model singel flip}\label{sect: P2E2}
In this exercise we perform a Monte Carlo simulation on a two-dimensional Ising model on a square lattice.\\
We use the following Hamiltonian without an external field and only looking at the spin in one direction with a constant interaction.

\begin{equation}
    H = -\frac{J}{2} \sum_{\left\langle i, j \right\rangle} S_i S_j
\end{equation}
Here J is the interaction strength with $J>0$ leading to a ferromagnet and $J<0$ to an antiferromagnet. $\left\langle i, j \right\rangle$ is the sum over nearest neighbours and corner neighbours. 
$S_i$ is the spin at site i with $S_i \in \{-1, +1\}$.\\
For the heat capacity we use the following expression:

\begin{equation}
    C_V = \frac{\left\langle E^2 \right\rangle - \left\langle E \right\rangle^2}{k_B T^2}.
\end{equation}
Here $\left\langle E^2 \right\rangle, \left\langle E \right\rangle^2$ are the average square energy and the square of the average energy respectively, $k_B$ the Boltzman constant and T the temperature. 

\subsection{$\left\langle S \right\rangle, \left\langle E \right\rangle, C_V$ and critical temperature}
We use a 100 x100 lattice and start of all our runs with all particles with spin up. We start with an energy of $-\frac{J}{2}\cdot 8 \cdot N^2 $ = -40000 and a magnetization of $N^2$ = 10000

We see that the system takes about a 100 steps maximum to reach equilibrium and has a critical temperature between $\beta = 0.1 $and $\beta = 0.4$\\
There are multiple equivalent ways to find the critical temperature: the maximum of the derivative of the magnetization or energy and the maximum of the heat capacity. 
We choose to find the critical temperature by finding the maximum of the heat capacity where we find $T_c = 5.3$. We plot this critical temperature as a vertical line in the 3 required plots where we see that the value agrees for all 3 plots.

For the Ising model with only nearest neighbours (excluding the corner neighbours) there is a known critical temperature of: $\frac{T_c}{J}= \frac{2}{log{(1 + \sqrt{2})}} \approx 2.269$ 
The value we found is roughly 3 higher which can intuitively be understood by the doubling of the nearest neighbours. Mean field theory gives an upper estimate of 8 ( the amount of nearest neighbours).Other models with 8 neighbours give values around 3.5, our critical temperature seems a bit high but internally consistent.
