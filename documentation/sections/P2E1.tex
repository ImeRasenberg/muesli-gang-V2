The energy as function of time can be found in Figure~\ref{fig: energy tau}. Here it can be seen that it has some equilibrating process taking place in the first $\approx 100$ time steps.

\begin{figure}[H]
	\centering

We don't yet know what a good choice is for dt, so we run the simulation many times with different choices for dt. Then we take the standard deviation of the energy in time. This is plotted in figure~\ref{fig: energy dt} 
\begin{figure}[H]
	\centering
	\includegraphics[width=0.5\linewidth]{graphs/P2E1/Energy_dt.png}
	\caption{The maximum change in energy as a function of step size}
	\label{fig: energy dt}
\end{figure}

We can see that taking time steps larger than $10^{-2}$ the simulations become nonsensical.
